Jamie Marsella, PhD

I am a historian of medicine, public health, gender, and religion in the modern United States.

My current project, Beyond Better Babies: Religion and Eugenic Maternalism in Progressive Era Child Welfare explores the role of domestic science, religion, and eugenic thought and practice in Progressive Era public health and child welfare. I center my analysis on a lesser-known movement aimed to complement eugenics by addressing the conditions in which women were having children and raising future generations by controlling the environment through hygiene, sanitation, and nutrition. I focus on the New York City Babiesâ€™ Welfare Association (BWA), a city-wide preventative care network for immigrant women and children made up of hundreds of philanthropic, religious, and city-funded organizations. In treating child welfare reformers as public health workers, my dissertation demonstrates the ways eugenic environmental reform permeated public health programs.

My research has been supported by fellowships from various institutions including the New York Academy of Medicine, the American Philosophical Society, Vassar College, the Consortium for the History of Science, Technology and Medicine, the Charles Warren Center for American Studies, and the Committee on the Study of Religion at Harvard University.

I hold a PhD from Harvard University, an MA from the University of Chicago, and a BA from the University of Portland. Prior to graduate school, I worked in digital marketing and public relations for various industries, including healthcare and education.
